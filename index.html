CITEC Capstone and Research Overview

Required steps for completing the final academic project.

ðŸŽ“ Capstone and IT Research Requirements

The Capstone track for the BS Information Technology program involves three core components: IT Research I (IT 311T), IT Research II (IT 401T), and the final Capstone Project (IT 406T).

IT Research 1 (IT 311T) - Proposal Phase

This course focuses on the foundational work for the capstone project. Key activities include:

  • **Problem Identification:** Defining the scope and specific issues to be addressed.
  • **Literature Review:** Conducting an extensive review of related literature and studies.
  • **Proposal Writing:** Completing and getting approval for the initial research proposal.

IT Research 2 (IT 401T) - Execution Phase

This course is dedicated to the successful execution of the approved research proposal:

  • **Implementation:** Developing and implementing the proposed solution or system.
  • **Data Activities:** Conducting data gathering and detailed analysis of the results.
  • **Paper Completion:** Writing and finalizing the full research paper.

Capstone Project (IT 406T) - Final Deliverable

This is the final phase where the research output is translated into a functional deliverable, focusing on system development and deployment based on the Research II output. The critical requirements include:

  • **Formal Approval:** The final proposal must be formally approved by the Research and Capstone Office and the CITEC Dean.
  • **Project Presentation:** Requires a successful final presentation of the working system to the Capstone/Thesis panel.
  • **Final Submission:** Submitting a hardbound copy of the completed research paper and all system documentation.

Academic Continuation Guidelines

Minimum marks and unit requirements to ensure satisfactory academic standing.

To ensure academic excellence and timely graduation, students must adhere to the following minimum standards (Note: Specific grades vary per semester and college policy; these are general university guidelines).

Requirement Minimum Mark Policy Detail
**Minimum Passing Mark (Per Subject)** **75%** (or Grade Point Equivalent of **3.0** or **P** for Pass) Any grade below this (e.g., 5.0 or 69%) is considered a failure and requires re-enrollment.
**General Weighted Average (GWA)** Minimum **2.75** (or specified GWA equivalent) Students must maintain the retention GWA per semester/year. Failure to do so may result in academic probation.
**Major Subject Minimum** Minimum **2.0** (or higher, e.g., 85%) For core professional subjects, a higher minimum may be enforced to proceed to more advanced, prerequisite-dependent courses.
**Maximum Failed Units** Not more than **9 Units** of failure per academic year. Exceeding the maximum allowed failed units can lead to academic disqualification or mandatory course shifting.

University of Batangas Student Handbook

Key policies, guidelines, and services for a bona fide UB student.

General Directives & Conduct

  • Identification Card: Students must always wear their ID while inside the campus. The ID is required to enter university grounds and must be presented to authorities upon request.
  • School Uniform: Students must wear the prescribed uniform with pride. For men: grayish-black pants, white polo, black leather belt, and necktie. For ladies: plaid maroon/black skirt, white blouse, and vest.
  • Wash Day / Casual Attire: On designated wash days, students may choose their clothes but must adhere to decency standards. Prohibited items include sleeveless shirts, ripped jeans, mini-skirts, and rubber slippers.
  • "Clean As You Go" (CLAYGO): Students are expected to clean up after themselves in the cafeteria and all campus areas.

Academic Policies

Policy Description
Grading System The University uses an eleven-point system.
1.00 - 1.25: Excellent (96-100)
3.00: Passing (75-77)
5.00: Failed.
Attendance A student will be dropped from a subject if absences reach 20% of the scheduled hours (lecture or laboratory). Late arrival of 15 minutes is considered one hour of absence.
Examinations Major exams follow the University Registrar's schedule. Students taking exams outside the schedule without a valid reason will be charged a special examination fee.
Adding/Dropping Adding subjects is allowed during a specified period. Dropping subjects can be done anytime before the final exam by securing an Adding/Dropping Form. Unofficial dropping results in a grade of 5.0.

Student Services

  • Counseling & Psychological Wellness Center (CoPWell): Offers mental health services, counseling, testing, and career coaching.
  • Health Services Office: Provides medical and dental examinations, first aid, and consultation. Open 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM on weekdays.
  • University Library: Features an open shelf system for easy access to resources. Students must present their ID to borrow materials.
  • International Student Services: Assists international students with visa processing and adjustment to university life.

Safety & Prohibitions

To maintain a safe environment, the following are strictly prohibited:

  • Dangerous Weapons: Firearms, knives, and explosives are strictly prohibited.
  • Illegal Drugs: Use, possession, or distribution of controlled substances is a violation of RA 9165 and university policy.
  • Hazing: Any form of hazing is strictly prohibited and subject to severe disciplinary action.
  • Gambling: Wagering or unauthorized games of chance are not allowed on premises.
